title: Story
---

Art never comes from happiness. It comes from the need to process what life puts you through — the memories that won't let go, the emotions too intense to stay inside, the moments that break you and somehow make you whole again.

My work is about emotional truth. I paint what I feel, not what I see. Cities, flowers, faces, objects — they all become vessels for something deeper: the weight of memory, the fragility of tenderness, the chaos of communication, the quiet strength of survival.

Each collection is a chapter. Each piece is a confession I didn't know how to say out loud.

Alena Mart is a contemporary artist from Eastern Europe whose work explores the complex terrain of human emotion through painting, drawing and mixed media.

Her artistic journey began in 2013 with her first exhibition in Minsk, featuring 30 works and attracting over a thousand visitors. Since then, she has developed a distinctive visual language that transforms personal experience into universal emotional statements.

Alena's work has been presented in exhibitions across Europe, including shows in Warsaw, Prague, Wroclaw and Italy. Her pieces are held in private collections internationally.

She works across multiple techniques — from oil painting and acrylics to pastels, gouache and the experimental grattage technique.
